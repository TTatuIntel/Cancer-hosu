Surgical Oncology

Surgical Oncology is a medical subspecialty that focuses on the diagnosis, staging, and treatment of cancer through surgery. It is often used in conjunction with other therapies/treatments for cancer. It focuses on the surgical management of tumors or other complications that arise in cancer treatment. A Surgical Oncologist (aka Onco surgeon) is a medical doctor who specializes in diagnosis and treatment of cancer through surgery.

Role of Surgery in Cancer Care

Diagnosis

To confirm a diagnosis of cancer, usually through taking a sample of tissue

Staging

To determine the stage of disease (extent of cancer spread) usually via minimally invasive procedures

Curative

To achieve cure by completely removing the diseased part of the body that is affected by cancer

Palliative

To relieve symptoms caused by the cancer for example obstruction, bleeding, pain

Reconstructive

To restore function and repair the damage done by cancer in the body

Myths about Cancer Surgery

1 Biopsies hasten the spread of cancer and must always be avoided

This is false. Modern biopsy techniques are designed to minimize any risk of cancer spread. Biopsies are essential diagnostic tools that help determine the appropriate treatment plan.

2 Cancer cannot be cured by surgery

This is a misconception. For many early-stage cancers, surgery can be curative, especially when the cancer is localized and has not spread to other parts of the body.

3 There is no role of surgery in management of early stages of cancer

On the contrary, surgery often plays a critical role in early-stage cancer treatment, offering the best chance for complete removal of the cancer before it spreads.

4 There is no difference between general surgery and cancer surgery

Surgical oncologists receive specialized training in cancer biology, multidisciplinary treatment approaches, and advanced surgical techniques specific to cancer treatment.

5 Cancer surgery always results in disability and disfigurement

Modern surgical techniques, including minimally invasive approaches and reconstructive procedures, aim to preserve function and appearance while effectively treating the cancer.

Medical Oncology

Medical Oncology is a specialty that focuses on treating cancer with medicines such as chemotherapy, targeted therapy, immunotherapy, and hormonal therapy. Medical oncologists are physicians who specialize in the diagnosis and treatment of cancer using these systemic treatments. They work closely with other specialists to develop comprehensive treatment plans tailored to each patient's specific cancer type and stage.

Types of Medical Oncology Treatments

Chemotherapy

Powerful drugs that kill rapidly dividing cells throughout the body, including cancer cells

Immunotherapy

Treatments that boost the body's natural defenses to fight cancer by helping the immune system recognize and attack cancer cells

Targeted Therapy

Drugs that target specific genes, proteins, or the tissue environment that contributes to cancer growth and survival

Hormone Therapy

Treatments that block or lower the amount of hormones in the body to slow or stop the growth of hormone-sensitive tumors

Precision Medicine

Tailoring treatment based on genetic understanding of a person's disease to optimize effectiveness and minimize side effects

Common Misconceptions about Medical Oncology

1 Chemotherapy is the only treatment option for cancer

While chemotherapy is a common treatment, medical oncology now includes many other approaches such as immunotherapy, targeted therapy, and hormone therapy that may be more appropriate depending on the cancer type.

2 All chemotherapy causes severe side effects

Modern chemotherapy regimens are often better tolerated than in the past. Side effects vary greatly depending on the specific drugs, dosage, and individual factors. Many side effects can be managed effectively.

3 Cancer treatments always make you lose your hair

Not all cancer treatments cause hair loss. Many targeted therapies and immunotherapies do not cause significant hair loss, and even with some chemotherapies, hair thinning rather than complete loss may occur.

4 Medical treatments for cancer are always worse than the disease

Modern cancer treatments are increasingly effective with manageable side effects. The goal is always to provide treatments where benefits outweigh risks, and quality of life is an important consideration.

5 Once you start treatment, you can never stop

Many cancer treatments have defined durations. Some patients may receive maintenance therapy, but others complete their treatment course and move to surveillance without ongoing medication.

Radiation Oncology

Radiation Oncology is a medical specialty that uses high-energy radiation to treat cancer by damaging the DNA of cancer cells, preventing them from dividing and growing. Radiation therapy can be delivered externally (external beam radiation) or internally (brachytherapy). Radiation oncologists are physicians who specialize in designing and overseeing radiation treatment plans for cancer patients.

Types of Radiation Therapy

External Beam Radiation

Radiation delivered from a machine outside the body, precisely targeting cancer cells while minimizing exposure to healthy tissue

Brachytherapy

Internal radiation therapy where radioactive sources are placed in or near the tumor, delivering a high dose of radiation to a specific area

Stereotactic Radiosurgery

Highly focused radiation beams used to treat small tumors with extreme precision, often in a single or few sessions

Proton Therapy

Advanced form of radiation that uses protons instead of x-rays, allowing for more precise targeting with less damage to surrounding tissues

Palliative Radiation

Radiation used to relieve symptoms and improve quality of life for patients with advanced cancer

Myths about Radiation Therapy

1 Radiation therapy makes you radioactive

External beam radiation therapy does not make patients radioactive. The radiation passes through the body and does not remain. With certain types of internal radiation, temporary precautions may be needed, but these are carefully managed by the medical team.

2 Radiation therapy always causes severe burns

While skin reactions can occur, modern radiation techniques have significantly reduced their severity. Most patients experience mild to moderate skin changes similar to sunburn, which typically heal within a few weeks after treatment.

3 Radiation therapy is painful during treatment

The actual delivery of radiation is painless - you don't feel the radiation beams. The treatment is similar to having an X-ray. Some patients may experience side effects that cause discomfort, but the treatment itself is not painful.

4 Radiation therapy always causes hair loss

Hair loss occurs only in the specific area being treated. For example, brain radiation may cause hair loss on the head, but breast radiation will not affect hair on the head.

5 Radiation therapy is dangerous to others around you

Patients receiving external beam radiation do not pose any radiation risk to family, friends, or caregivers. The radiation does not remain in the body after treatment sessions.

Pediatric Oncology

Pediatric Oncology is a medical specialty focused on the diagnosis and treatment of cancer in children and adolescents. Pediatric oncologists are specially trained to address the unique needs of young patients, considering their developing bodies, emotional well-being, and long-term health. Treatment approaches are tailored to minimize long-term effects while maximizing cure rates.

Common Childhood Cancers

Leukemia

The most common childhood cancer, affecting the blood and bone marrow, with acute lymphoblastic leukemia (ALL) being the most prevalent type

Brain Tumors

The second most common childhood cancer, with various types affecting different parts of the brain and requiring specialized treatment approaches

Lymphoma

Cancer of the lymphatic system, including Hodgkin lymphoma and non-Hodgkin lymphoma, affecting the immune system

Neuroblastoma

A cancer that develops from immature nerve cells, typically affecting children under 5 years of age

Wilms Tumor

A kidney cancer that primarily affects children under 5 years of age, often presenting as an abdominal mass

Myths about Childhood Cancer

1 Childhood cancer is contagious

Cancer is never contagious. Children with cancer can interact normally with other children and adults without any risk of spreading their disease.

2 Children can't survive cancer

With modern treatments, the overall 5-year survival rate for childhood cancers is over 80%. Many childhood cancers are highly curable, especially when detected early.

3 Children with cancer should be kept isolated

While children undergoing treatment may need to avoid crowds during periods of low immunity, social interaction is crucial for their emotional well-being. Most children can continue school and normal activities with appropriate precautions.

4 Children are too young to understand their diagnosis

Children often understand more than adults realize. Age-appropriate explanations about their illness help children cope better and build trust with their healthcare team.

5 Childhood cancer is caused by something parents did wrong

The causes of most childhood cancers are unknown and not related to parental actions or environmental factors. Parents should never blame themselves for their child's diagnosis.

Medical Oncology

Medical Oncology is a specialty that focuses on treating cancer with medicines such as chemotherapy, targeted therapy, immunotherapy, and hormonal therapy. Medical oncologists are physicians who specialize in the diagnosis and treatment of cancer using these systemic treatments. They work closely with other specialists to develop comprehensive treatment plans tailored to each patient's specific cancer type and stage.

Types of Medical Oncology Treatments

Chemotherapy

Powerful drugs that kill rapidly dividing cells throughout the body, including cancer cells

Immunotherapy

Treatments that boost the body's natural defenses to fight cancer by helping the immune system recognize and attack cancer cells

Targeted Therapy

Drugs that target specific genes, proteins, or the tissue environment that contributes to cancer growth and survival

Hormone Therapy

Treatments that block or lower the amount of hormones in the body to slow or stop the growth of hormone-sensitive tumors

Precision Medicine

Tailoring treatment based on genetic understanding of a person's disease to optimize effectiveness and minimize side effects

Common Misconceptions about Medical Oncology

1 Chemotherapy is the only treatment option for cancer

While chemotherapy is a common treatment, medical oncology now includes many other approaches such as immunotherapy, targeted therapy, and hormone therapy that may be more appropriate depending on the cancer type.

2 All chemotherapy causes severe side effects

Modern chemotherapy regimens are often better tolerated than in the past. Side effects vary greatly depending on the specific drugs, dosage, and individual factors. Many side effects can be managed effectively.

3 Cancer treatments always make you lose your hair

Not all cancer treatments cause hair loss. Many targeted therapies and immunotherapies do not cause significant hair loss, and even with some chemotherapies, hair thinning rather than complete loss may occur.

4 Medical treatments for cancer are always worse than the disease

Modern cancer treatments are increasingly effective with manageable side effects. The goal is always to provide treatments where benefits outweigh risks, and quality of life is an important consideration.

5 Once you start treatment, you can never stop

Many cancer treatments have defined durations. Some patients may receive maintenance therapy, but others complete their treatment course and move to surveillance without ongoing medication.

Radiation Oncology

Radiation Oncology is a medical specialty that uses high-energy radiation to treat cancer by damaging the DNA of cancer cells, preventing them from dividing and growing. Radiation therapy can be delivered externally (external beam radiation) or internally (brachytherapy). Radiation oncologists are physicians who specialize in designing and overseeing radiation treatment plans for cancer patients.

Types of Radiation Therapy

External Beam Radiation

Radiation delivered from a machine outside the body, precisely targeting cancer cells while minimizing exposure to healthy tissue

Brachytherapy

Internal radiation therapy where radioactive sources are placed in or near the tumor, delivering a high dose of radiation to a specific area

Stereotactic Radiosurgery

Highly focused radiation beams used to treat small tumors with extreme precision, often in a single or few sessions

Proton Therapy

Advanced form of radiation that uses protons instead of x-rays, allowing for more precise targeting with less damage to surrounding tissues

Palliative Radiation

Radiation used to relieve symptoms and improve quality of life for patients with advanced cancer

Myths about Radiation Therapy

1 Radiation therapy makes you radioactive

External beam radiation therapy does not make patients radioactive. The radiation passes through the body and does not remain. With certain types of internal radiation, temporary precautions may be needed, but these are carefully managed by the medical team.

2 Radiation therapy always causes severe burns

While skin reactions can occur, modern radiation techniques have significantly reduced their severity. Most patients experience mild to moderate skin changes similar to sunburn, which typically heal within a few weeks after treatment.

3 Radiation therapy is painful during treatment

The actual delivery of radiation is painless - you don't feel the radiation beams. The treatment is similar to having an X-ray. Some patients may experience side effects that cause discomfort, but the treatment itself is not painful.

4 Radiation therapy always causes hair loss

Hair loss occurs only in the specific area being treated. For example, brain radiation may cause hair loss on the head, but breast radiation will not affect hair on the head.

5 Radiation therapy is dangerous to others around you

Patients receiving external beam radiation do not pose any radiation risk to family, friends, or caregivers. The radiation does not remain in the body after treatment sessions.

Pediatric Oncology

Pediatric Oncology is a medical specialty focused on the diagnosis and treatment of cancer in children and adolescents. Pediatric oncologists are specially trained to address the unique needs of young patients, considering their developing bodies, emotional well-being, and long-term health. Treatment approaches are tailored to minimize long-term effects while maximizing cure rates.

Common Childhood Cancers

Leukemia

The most common childhood cancer, affecting the blood and bone marrow, with acute lymphoblastic leukemia (ALL) being the most prevalent type

Brain Tumors

The second most common childhood cancer, with various types affecting different parts of the brain and requiring specialized treatment approaches

Lymphoma

Cancer of the lymphatic system, including Hodgkin lymphoma and non-Hodgkin lymphoma, affecting the immune system

Neuroblastoma

A cancer that develops from immature nerve cells, typically affecting children under 5 years of age

Wilms Tumor

A kidney cancer that primarily affects children under 5 years of age, often presenting as an abdominal mass

Myths about Childhood Cancer

1 Childhood cancer is contagious

Cancer is never contagious. Children with cancer can interact normally with other children and adults without any risk of spreading their disease.

2 Children can't survive cancer

With modern treatments, the overall 5-year survival rate for childhood cancers is over 80%. Many childhood cancers are highly curable, especially when detected early.

3 Children with cancer should be kept isolated

While children undergoing treatment may need to avoid crowds during periods of low immunity, social interaction is crucial for their emotional well-being. Most children can continue school and normal activities with appropriate precautions.

4 Children are too young to understand their diagnosis

Children often understand more than adults realize. Age-appropriate explanations about their illness help children cope better and build trust with their healthcare team.

5 Childhood cancer is caused by something parents did wrong

The causes of most childhood cancers are unknown and not related to parental actions or environmental factors. Parents should never blame themselves for their child's diagnosis.

Surgical Oncology

Surgical Oncology is a medical subspecialty that focuses on the diagnosis, staging, and treatment of cancer through surgery. It is often used in conjunction with other therapies/treatments for cancer. It focuses on the surgical management of tumors or other complications that arise in cancer treatment. A Surgical Oncologist (aka Onco surgeon) is a medical doctor who specializes in diagnosis and treatment of cancer through surgery.

Role of Surgery in Cancer Care

Diagnosis

To confirm a diagnosis of cancer, usually through taking a sample of tissue

Staging

To determine the stage of disease (extent of cancer spread) usually via minimally invasive procedures

Curative

To achieve cure by completely removing the diseased part of the body that is affected by cancer

Palliative

To relieve symptoms caused by the cancer for example obstruction, bleeding, pain

Reconstructive

To restore function and repair the damage done by cancer in the body

Myths about Cancer Surgery

1 Biopsies hasten the spread of cancer and must always be avoided

This is false. Modern biopsy techniques are designed to minimize any risk of cancer spread. Biopsies are essential diagnostic tools that help determine the appropriate treatment plan.

2 Cancer cannot be cured by surgery

This is a misconception. For many early-stage cancers, surgery can be curative, especially when the cancer is localized and has not spread to other parts of the body.

3 There is no role of surgery in management of early stages of cancer

On the contrary, surgery often plays a critical role in early-stage cancer treatment, offering the best chance for complete removal of the cancer before it spreads.

4 There is no difference between general surgery and cancer surgery

Surgical oncologists receive specialized training in cancer biology, multidisciplinary treatment approaches, and advanced surgical techniques specific to cancer treatment.

5 Cancer surgery always results in disability and disfigurement

Modern surgical techniques, including minimally invasive approaches and reconstructive procedures, aim to preserve function and appearance while effectively treating the cancer.
