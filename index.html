Surgical Oncology

Surgical Oncology is a medical subspecialty that focuses on the diagnosis, staging, and treatment of cancer through surgery. It is often used in conjunction with other therapies/treatments for cancer. It focuses on the surgical management of tumors or other complications that arise in cancer treatment. A Surgical Oncologist (aka Onco surgeon) is a medical doctor who specializes in diagnosis and treatment of cancer through surgery.

Role of Surgery in Cancer Care

Diagnosis

To confirm a diagnosis of cancer, usually through taking a sample of tissue

Staging

To determine the stage of disease (extent of cancer spread) usually via minimally invasive procedures

Curative

To achieve cure by completely removing the diseased part of the body that is affected by cancer

Palliative

To relieve symptoms caused by the cancer for example obstruction, bleeding, pain

Reconstructive

To restore function and repair the damage done by cancer in the body

Myths about Cancer Surgery

1 Biopsies hasten the spread of cancer and must always be avoided

This is false. Modern biopsy techniques are designed to minimize any risk of cancer spread. Biopsies are essential diagnostic tools that help determine the appropriate treatment plan.

2 Cancer cannot be cured by surgery

This is a misconception. For many early-stage cancers, surgery can be curative, especially when the cancer is localized and has not spread to other parts of the body.

3 There is no role of surgery in management of early stages of cancer

On the contrary, surgery often plays a critical role in early-stage cancer treatment, offering the best chance for complete removal of the cancer before it spreads.

4 There is no difference between general surgery and cancer surgery

Surgical oncologists receive specialized training in cancer biology, multidisciplinary treatment approaches, and advanced surgical techniques specific to cancer treatment.

5 Cancer surgery always results in disability and disfigurement

Modern surgical techniques, including minimally invasive approaches and reconstructive procedures, aim to preserve function and appearance while effectively treating the cancer.

Medical Oncology

Medical Oncology is a specialty that focuses on treating cancer with medicines such as chemotherapy, targeted therapy, immunotherapy, and hormonal therapy. Medical oncologists are physicians who specialize in the diagnosis and treatment of cancer using these systemic treatments. They work closely with other specialists to develop comprehensive treatment plans tailored to each patient's specific cancer type and stage.

Types of Medical Oncology Treatments

Chemotherapy

Powerful drugs that kill rapidly dividing cells throughout the body, including cancer cells

Immunotherapy

Treatments that boost the body's natural defenses to fight cancer by helping the immune system recognize and attack cancer cells

Targeted Therapy

Drugs that target specific genes, proteins, or the tissue environment that contributes to cancer growth and survival

Hormone Therapy

Treatments that block or lower the amount of hormones in the body to slow or stop the growth of hormone-sensitive tumors

Precision Medicine

Tailoring treatment based on genetic understanding of a person's disease to optimize effectiveness and minimize side effects

Radiation Oncology

Radiation Oncology is a medical specialty that uses high-energy radiation to treat cancer. Radiation oncologists employ precise techniques to target and destroy cancer cells while sparing healthy tissue. This treatment can be used alone or in combination with surgery and medical oncology to improve outcomes for patients with various types of cancer.

Types of Radiation Therapy

External Beam Radiation

Delivers radiation from a machine outside the body, targeting the tumor with high precision

Brachytherapy

Involves placing radioactive sources inside or near the tumor to deliver a high dose of radiation directly

Stereotactic Radiosurgery

High-dose radiation delivered in a single or few sessions to treat small tumors with extreme precision

Proton Therapy

Uses protons instead of X-rays to deliver radiation, allowing for precise targeting with less damage to surrounding tissues

Image-Guided Radiation Therapy

Uses imaging during treatment to improve the precision and accuracy of radiation delivery

Pediatric Oncology

Pediatric Oncology focuses on the diagnosis and treatment of cancers in children and adolescents. Pediatric oncologists specialize in managing cancers such as leukemia, brain tumors, and sarcomas, using a combination of surgery, chemotherapy, radiation, and other therapies tailored to young patients to maximize outcomes and minimize long-term effects.

Key Aspects of Pediatric Oncology

Leukemia Treatment

Uses chemotherapy and sometimes stem cell transplants to treat cancers of the blood and bone marrow

Brain Tumor Management

Combines surgery, radiation, and chemotherapy to address tumors in the brain and spinal cord

Sarcoma Care

Focuses on treating cancers of the bone and soft tissues with a multidisciplinary approach

Supportive Care

Provides psychological and social support to children and families to improve quality of life during treatment

Long-Term Follow-Up

Monitors survivors for late effects of treatment to ensure healthy development into adulthood
